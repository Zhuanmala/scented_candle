# Sample Test Protocol

Purpose: test supplier samples before any production order.

## Safety Note

Burn tests should be done in a controlled indoor environment on a heat-resistant surface, away from drafts, children, pets, textiles, and flammable objects. Never leave a burning candle unattended.

## Sample Intake

For every sample, record:
- Supplier.
- Sample code.
- Wax type.
- Wax weight.
- Wick type.
- Container material.
- Fragrance name.
- Batch/sample date if available.
- Documents received.

## Visual Inspection

Score 1-5:
- Wax surface smoothness.
- Container quality.
- Label alignment.
- Box quality.
- Premium feel.
- Cheap/plastic feel reversed score.

Reject if:
- Container is scratched/cracked.
- Wax is badly cracked or full of bubbles.
- Label is visibly crooked.
- Packaging is visibly cheap.

## Cold Scent Test

Record before burning:
- First impression.
- Scent family.
- Strength 1-5.
- Premium feel 1-5.
- Cheap synthetic feel 1-5.
- Is it giftable?
- What German price does it suggest visually and olfactively?

## Burn Test

Test in intervals:

1. First 15 minutes:
   - Flame stability.
   - Smoke.
   - Wick centering.
   - Immediate scent throw.

2. First 1 hour:
   - Wax pool.
   - Tunneling.
   - Container heat.
   - Soot.
   - Scent character changes.

3. First 2-3 hours:
   - Full melt pool behavior.
   - Any mushrooming.
   - Any overheating.
   - Any unpleasant off-note.

4. Extinguish:
   - Smoke after extinguishing.
   - Irritating smell.
   - Wick condition.

Reject if:
- Serious black smoke.
- Container becomes abnormally hot.
- Flame unstable or too large.
- Severe tunneling in normal use.
- Crack, pop, or unsafe behavior.
- Strong unpleasant smell after extinguishing.

## Final Sample Decision

Decision labels:
- `Advance to blind test`.
- `Supplier follow-up needed`.
- `Reject scent only`.
- `Reject supplier`.

## Photo Requirements

Take photos:
- Before burning.
- Label close-up.
- Box/container.
- After 15 minutes.
- After 1 hour.
- After 2-3 hours.
- After extinguishing.
